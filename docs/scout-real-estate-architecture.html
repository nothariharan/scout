1. What Scout Is

Scout is a voice-AI agent that removes the most painful part of finding a place to live or buy: the endless cycle of calling brokers, landlords, PG managers, and hostel wardens, repeating the same requirements, and never knowing if the price you're quoted is fair.

Instead of a human doing this themselves, Scout runs a structured "interview" with the user to understand exactly what they want, then dispatches a fleet of AI voice agents to call every relevant listing in the target area in parallel — negotiating price, verifying legitimacy, and returning a ranked, side-by-side comparison of real offers, not just listings.

Think of it as hiring a tireless, incorruptible personal real estate broker who works 24/7, speaks every local language, never gets tired of haggling, and has no incentive to push you toward a specific property (no commission bias).

Non-binding verbal quotes"Rent is ₹15,000 approx" turns into ₹18,000 once you show up, with maintenance and "society charges" added later.

Module 1 — Intake ("The Interview")

The first thing a user sees is not a search bar — it's a conversation. A clean, Apple-style interface (minimal, generous whitespace, one question at a time, large tap targets) where the user is "interviewed" by an AI concierge (text or voice) that behaves like a personal registrar/manager, not a form.

  • Deal type: Rent (long-term), PG/hostel (short stay), buy, or lease-to-buy.
  • Location & radius: Pinned via Google Maps; user can set a target area or a commute-time constraint ("under 20 min from X office").
  • Budget range: Hard ceiling vs "ideal" price, and whether they want Scout to negotiate below the ceiling or just verify fairness.
  • Specs: Bedrooms/occupancy, furnishing level, amenities (parking, wifi, food included, gym, security), move-in date, lease duration.
  • Document readiness: "Do you have ID proof / income proof / company letter ready?" — flags which listings will accept the user's paperwork profile instantly.
  • Deal-breakers: No smoking building, pet-friendly required, female-only PG, etc.
  • Negotiation posture: Aggressive (maximize savings, slower), balanced, or fast (accept first fair deal).

Output of Module 1: a structured JSON "requirement spec" — this is the single source of truth every outbound call will reference, so the user never repeats themselves again.

Module 2 — Discovery & Parallel Negotiation ("The Calling Engine")

This is Scout's core differentiator: it doesn't just list properties, it calls on them.

  • Discovery layer: Pulls candidate listings from Google Maps Places API, real-estate portal scrapes/APIs (99acres, MagicBricks, NoBroker, Zillow-equivalents depending on geography), and broker/PG directories within the target radius.
  • Geofencing filter: Immediately drops anything outside the commute/distance constraint before a single call is placed — this is where the "nearby real estate" Google Maps check happens, so Scout doesn't waste calls on out-of-range properties.
  • Parallel outbound calling: Dozens of calls fired simultaneously via Twilio (or regional telephony provider), each running the same structured spec from Module 1.
  • Multilingual negotiation agents: Scout detects the language the person on the other end responds in and can dynamically switch the ElevenLabs conversational voice agent to that language/dialect. A separate, more "haggling-tuned" agent persona (assertive, price-anchoring, comfortable with back-and-forth) is used specifically for the negotiation phase, distinct from the friendly intake persona.
  • Live negotiation logic: Agent opens with the requirement spec, asks for the real (not listed) price, cross-references it against Scout's internal pricing benchmark for that micro-area, and counters using competing offers ("we have another unit nearby at ₹X") as leverage — without ever lying about specifics it can't verify.
  • Fraud & identity checks during the call: Agent asks for property registration/RERA number (India) or equivalent, cross-checks the caller's claimed identity against listing platform data, and flags any request for "pre-visit deposit" as high-risk (a very common real estate scam pattern).
  • Retry & follow-up: No-answer or "call back later" outcomes are automatically re-queued; ghosting brokers get a scheduled follow-up call without user involvement.

Module 3 — Comparison, Verification & Recommendation

  • Normalized comparison table: All quotes converted into one standard format — base rent/price, deposit, maintenance, brokerage, included amenities, effective total monthly/total cost.
  • Map view: Every viable result pinned on a map with commute time overlay, so price and location trade-offs are visual, not just numeric.
  • Fraud/risk score: Each listing gets a risk badge — Verified, Caution, or High Risk — based on registration checks, listing-platform cross-references, deposit-request behavior, and inconsistencies between call transcript and listing description.
  • Recommendation with reasoning: Not just "cheapest" — a ranked shortlist with an explanation ("This one is 8% above your budget but 12 min closer and verified; this one is cheapest but flagged for pre-visit deposit request").
  • Call transcripts & recordings: User can listen to or read exactly what was negotiated, building trust in what the AI did on their behalf.
  • One-tap next step: Schedule a site visit, request a virtual video walkthrough, or move straight to a re-negotiation round with the top 2-3 finalists.

4. Fraud & Risk System (Dedicated Layer)

Given how scam-prone real estate is, this isn't a feature bolted onto Module 3 — it should be a cross-cutting layer that touches every module.

SignalHow Scout Detects It
Fake/duplicate listingsCross-reference address, photos, and price against multiple portals; flag listings that appear identical across "different" owners.
Pre-visit deposit requestsNegotiation agent is trained to treat any request for payment before a verified visit/contract as an automatic red flag — never authorized to pay or commit funds under any circumstance.
Unregistered propertyAsk for and validate registration/RERA-style IDs where applicable; absence isn't disqualifying but lowers the trust score.
Identity mismatchCompare the name/entity on the call to the name on the listing platform and property records where public data exists.
Pressure tacticsNLP layer on the transcript flags urgency/scarcity language ("only today", "someone else is paying now") common in scam scripts.
Community signalsAggregate anonymized outcomes across all Scout users — a broker/owner who scams one user gets flagged for all future users automatically (the network-effect data moat).
Legal note: Scout's agent must never be authorized to transfer money, sign anything, or make a binding verbal commitment on the user's behalf. Its role is strictly informational/negotiational — final commitment always requires explicit user action.

5. UI / UX Principles

  • Conversation-first, not form-first: One question at a time, natural language allowed as an escape hatch ("just tell me what you need and I'll fill in the form").
  • Apple-like minimalism: Generous whitespace, large legible type, few colors, motion used sparingly to indicate progress (e.g. a live "Scout is calling 14 properties near you..." progress state).
  • Transparency by default: Every AI action (a call placed, a counteroffer made) should be visible in a live activity feed, not hidden — this directly targets the "will I trust an AI to negotiate for me" hurdle.
  • Listen-in option: Let anxious users optionally listen to a call live or immediately after, especially valuable for older or first-time users.
  • Map as a first-class citizen: Since location is central to real estate (unlike moving/plumbing verticals), the map view should be as prominent as the comparison table, not an afterthought.

7. MVP Feature List (Build First)

  • Structured conversational intake (text-based to start, voice later)
  • Google Maps radius + commute-time filtering
  • Parallel outbound calling to a first batch (5–10) of listings/PGs in one city
  • Single-language negotiation agent (expand to multilingual post-MVP)
  • Basic quote normalization + comparison table
  • Manual fraud checklist (registration ID ask, deposit-request flag) before full automated scoring
  • Call transcript log visible to user

8. Post-MVP / Future Features

  • Multilingual auto-switching negotiation agent (detect language mid-call and swap persona)
  • Automated fraud scoring engine with cross-portal duplicate detection
  • Live "listen-in" mode for ongoing calls
  • Virtual walkthrough scheduling (video call with landlord/PG owner arranged automatically)
  • Pricing benchmark dashboard, publicly shareable ("average 1BHK rent in this pincode") to build trust and SEO
  • Recurring/subscription mode for property managers or relocation agencies managing many searches at once
  • Document parsing: user uploads a rental agreement or listing screenshot; AI extracts terms automatically
  • Re-negotiation rounds: after initial comparison, auto-trigger a second round of calls to the top 2–3 finalists using competing offers as leverage
  • API/white-label version for relocation companies, corporate HR (employee housing), and student housing platforms

9. Legal & Compliance Notes (Real Estate Specific)

  • AI-call disclosure required upfront in many jurisdictions — Scout's agent should state it's an automated assistant calling on behalf of a client.
  • Call recording consent laws vary by region/state — must be configured per geography.
  • The AI agent must never be authorized to sign, commit funds, or make binding verbal agreements — always "subject to client confirmation."
  • Real estate is often separately regulated (brokerage licensing laws in some regions) — confirm Scout's role is "informational negotiation assistant," not acting as a licensed broker, to stay out of regulated territory initially.
  • Data privacy: user financial/document details (income proof, ID) must be handled under strict encryption and regional privacy law (GDPR, local equivalents).

10. Why This Wins

  • Real estate is one of the highest-stakes, highest-anxiety purchase categories that still runs almost entirely on phone calls and word-of-mouth — ripe for the exact gap Scout fills.
  • The fraud/scam angle is uniquely severe in real estate compared to other verticals (moving, plumbing), making the risk-scoring layer a genuine differentiator, not just a nice-to-have.
  • Location is a first-class variable here in a way it isn't for other verticals — Scout's Maps-native filtering is a natural fit and hard for generic voice-AI platforms to replicate.
  • Every negotiated call adds to a proprietary pricing-benchmark dataset — the longer Scout runs in a city, the smarter and faster its negotiation gets, creating a compounding data moat.
